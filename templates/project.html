    AUTOMATED WAVELET ANALYSIS OF LOW RESOLUTION GAMMA-RAY SPECTRA AND PEAK AREA UNCERTAINTY

    The accuracy of automated isotope identification from low resolution gamma-ray spectra can be significantly improved with better algorithms. The method based on the wavelet transform and non-negative least squares (NNLS) is introduced here.

    Two different spectra (top) with the same Fourier transform (bottom).

    Example of STFT with different window length.

    Noise level determined from coefficient at the smallest scale.

    Simple simulated tests of the wavelet algorithm. The blue line represents the peak and the red bar represents the calculated peak area from the NNLS fitting.

    Comparison of calculated area uncertainty between NNLS (blue) and software OriginLab (red). The blue curve was calculated by the wavelet algorithm and red curve by OriginLab. Both gave similar results and it could be seen that the larger the signal to noise ratio (SNR), the smaller the area uncertainty

    In the left figure, four local maxima were found in the scalogram of Cs-137, corresponding to the four peaks: (1) backscatter peak; (2) photopeak of Cs-137 at 662 keV; (3) background peak of K-40 at 1460 keV and (4) background 36peak of Tl-208 at 2614 keV.
